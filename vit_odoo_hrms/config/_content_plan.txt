1. Project Overview & Content Goals
EmPay is an AI-assisted Human Resource Management (HRM) system built on the Odoo framework. Designed for modern teams, it combines payroll processing, attendance tracking, employee self-service, and HR analytics inside a clean, purple-branded interface. This document defines every piece of written content needed to ship a polished, submission-ready product within the 24-hour hackathon window.
1.1  Core Content Objectives
Deliver zero-ambiguity UI copy across all screens — labels, buttons, tooltips, empty states, and error messages.
Establish a consistent brand voice that feels professional yet approachable, matching the Odoo design language.
Provide ready-to-use email and notification templates so the demo showcases end-to-end HR workflows.
Create concise onboarding text and in-app guidance so judges can navigate the system without assistance.
Ensure all content is accessible, gender-neutral, and free from jargon.

8. Tone of Voice Guidelines
8.1  Brand Voice in Four Words
Clear · Human · Confident · Efficient
8.2  Do / Don't Matrix
✅ Do
❌ Don't
Use active voice: 'Your payslip is ready'
Use passive voice: 'The payslip has been generated'
Be specific: 'You have 3 pending leave requests'
Be vague: 'Some items need your attention'
Use plain language: 'Days off' over 'Absence allocation'
Use jargon: 'Deductible remuneration component'
Acknowledge user actions: 'Payslip confirmed'
Leave confirmations silent or technical
Use gender-neutral language: 'they/their'
Use gendered defaults: 'his/her'
8.3  Numeric & Date Formatting
Currency: ₹ 1,25,000.00 (Indian number system for demo; configurable per locale)
Dates: DD Mon YYYY (e.g., 01 May 2026) for display; DD/MM/YYYY in input fields
Percentages: 12.5% (no space between number and symbol)
Large numbers: Use comma separators (1,25,000 — Indian standard)
9. Accessibility & Localisation Notes
9.1  Accessibility Checklist
All icon-only buttons must have an aria-label or screen-reader text equivalent.
Colour must not be the sole indicator of status — always pair with text label.
Error messages must be announced by screen readers (use role='alert').
Form fields must have associated <label> elements, not just placeholder text.
Avoid emoji as the sole conveyor of information in labels or status badges.
9.2  Localisation Flags for Future Sprints
All user-facing strings should be wrapped in translation keys (t() in Odoo's i18n system) — no hardcoded text in templates.
Date and currency formats are locale-aware; default to en_IN for hackathon demo.
Right-to-left (RTL) layout is not in scope for this sprint but architecture should support it.
